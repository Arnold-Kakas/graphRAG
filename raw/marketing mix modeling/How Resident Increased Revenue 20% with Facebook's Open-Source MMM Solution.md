### Resident, an e-commerce company, has digitally native home goods brands including Nectar and DreamCloud. It sells its products, primarily mattresses, through digital channels in the US and UK and at more than 2,500 brick-and-mortar stores in the US. In 2020, COVID-19 had a major impact on both Resident and the industry more broadly as consumers switched from offline to online. This led to increases in revenue and other business metrics.

### Introduction

The company had also already been looking ahead to upcoming privacy changes in the digital landscape, understanding these could have a negative impact on its marketing measurement.

Seeing the change in its business during 2020, understanding the looming changes to both the quality and quantity of its marketing measurement signals and knowing that it was planning on increasing offline media spending, Resident decided to pursue a marketing mix modeling (MMM) solution for its marketing measurement.

The process began with an in-house solution created by its data science team and soon included Facebook's then-brand-new open-source MMM initiative Robyn. From there, Resident and Facebook worked together on Facebook's new open-source solution comparing and iterating both measurement models.

At the end of 2020, Resident began using MMM—with both its in-house model and Facebook's Robyn—for its monthly budget allocations. This enabled better marketing spend decisions and more profitable choices, helping to drive a 20% quarter-over-quarter jump in the first half of 2021 in US revenue growth while maintaining a steady blended cost per acquisition.

### The Challenge

Resident is a pure direct response company that judges every marketing dollar spent based on return on ad spend and blended cost per acquisition across its complex marketing mix.

For its monthly budget allocation decisions, Resident used an in-house attribution model using a "first click" model for prospecting activity and "last click" for retargeting. This model made it difficult for Resident to fully understand the value of its entire media mix, including its Facebook marketing. This relatively complex sale of its high-ticket items included a multi-stage sales journey, and Resident knew its current attribution model often was creating duplicates when counting conversions.

Understanding the growing importance of digital to its business coupled with privacy changes in the online advertising ecosystem and knowing it was planning on increasing its spending in offline media meant Resident had the need for a robust measurement solution. This new solution would need to measure different marketing channels and serve as a transparent "source of truth" within Resident while being resilient to upcoming privacy changes, avoiding the conversion duplication issue and accurately measuring both offline and online marketing channels.

To begin the process, Resident allocated internal resources to its data science team to develop a MMM solution. But because developing MMM in-house is resource-heavy, it took the initial model five working months to implement and didn't include Facebook best practices. Knowing this, Facebook's Marketing Science team approached Resident to partner on a measurement program using Facebook's then-brand-new open-source MMM initiative Robyn.

### The Solution

Resident had its in-house solution built by its data science team and had already determined MMM was its path forward for measuring its different marketing channels and better optimizing its monthly budget allocations. Knowing Resident's interest in MMM, Facebook's Marketing Science team educated Resident about its then unannounced open-source MMM initiative.

The team demonstrated that Robyn offered a built-in ability to adhere to Facebook's best practices and that its code could be customized with Resident's requirements, and they provided a technical overview of Robyn's features.

After this meeting, the Resident and Facebook teams worked together on a comprehensive comparison of Resident's in-house MMM solution and Facebook's Robyn solution. This comparison found some Robyn features that were not included in Resident's initial solution:

- Automatic tuning of hyperparameters;
- Embedded lift results that ranked selected models closer to true value;
- An easier to implement DMA-level model due to Robyn's infrastructure; and
- Robyn was found to be less computation-heavy and was faster to run.

Resident began running both MMM solutions in December 2020 using Facebook's Robyn to validate its own in-house solution and to determine if the two solutions were aligned in the recommendations provided and results returned. This ongoing validation found similar results between both solutions and led to multiple iterations for each.

### The Impact

After adopting MMM as its measurement source of truth for monthly budget allocation decisions, Resident's overall (all products) US revenue grew 20% quarter-over-quarter, and the company was able to maintain the same blended cost per acquisition it had before the shift in its measurement approach. Using MMM provided Resident with insights into its marketing mix that drove profitable budget choices.

Working with Facebook's Robyn open-source MMM solution created a trust between the companies. This partnership led to a shift in advertising budget allocations based on MMM results which created a 15 to 20% lift in Facebook's share of Resident's overall media budget while maintaining the same blended cost per acquisition.

This trust also led Resident to implement the newer version of the open-source solution—Robyn 2.0—after learning about benefits included in the new iteration of the solution such as support for calibration and granularity.

Resident's success with its new measurement approach is notable among direct response companies. Many are looking for success stories to understand the challenge of implementing MMM and using it for measurement to determine whether budget allocation using MMM is with the return on investment. The partnership between Resident and Facebook helped foster this success and confidence in the new approach. And, as an early adopter of Robyn, Resident helped Facebook with feature development in the open-source project.

Currently, Resident continues to use both its in-house MMM solution and Facebook's Robyn solution together on a monthly basis. MMM has become an anchor in Resident's decision-making process and the company is committed to continue its investment in the measurement approach. Resident is planning on using only Facebook's Robyn as its MMM solution in the near future.

***"The involvement of the Facebook team in Resident's marketing mix modeling contributed a lot to the success of this project. Adding marketing mix modeling as an additional measurement tool was crucial since measuring the impact of Facebook ads, as well as ad exposure on other channels, is a real challenge using click-based methods."***

Avi Shenshakir, Vice President of Data, Resident

### Key Takeaways

- ###### The early stage of a project is critical for strategic decision making:
	Because Resident began working with Facebook early in its MMM journey it was easy to incorporate Facebook best practices in its new measurement approach, something that would be more difficult later in the process.

- ###### Collaboration wins the day:
	By working together, Resident and Facebook were able to push each other with Facebook helping Resident as it transitioned to an MMM approach and Resident providing feedback that improved Facebook's Robyn initiative.

- ###### Start, learn and iterate:
	In this case, both Resident and Facebook had nascent MMM solutions that allowed the two companies to work together while learning, improving and then repeating the process.
