### Founded in 2012 and headquartered in New York, BARK is a dog-centric company devoted to serving dogs in four key categories: fun, food, health, and home. BARK offers several monthly subscription services, including BarkBox, Super Chewer, BARK Bright, and BARK Eats, with more than 2.3 million active subscriptions. BARK also offers its products for sale via direct-to-dog-parent channels, online marketplaces and a network of retailers.

### The Challenge

BARK wanted a cost-efficient way to measure the effectiveness of its marketing spend and performance across channels, and determine the optimal budget allocation mix. The dog-centric company tested Robyn, Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) code from Meta Open Source, which showed that properly optimized budget allocation can generate a 30% increase in the brand’s overall subscription sales.

### The Solution

BARK sought to evaluate its customer acquisition costs in a consistent manner across channels, including Meta, Google, email, linear TV and Amazon. It also wanted to understand the impact that non-spend variables such as press releases, news blurbs, BARK’s website content and promotional offerings have on driving subscriptions.

BARK’s lead data scientist was really excited to discover Robyn, Meta Open Source’s MMM solution, which is a cross-channel measurement tool that enables advertisers to take ad spend input from their digital and traditional channels, see marketing investment efficiency across those channels and identify where their dollars are, and are not, being effective.

Developed by Meta’s marketing science team, Robyn’s open source code democratizes access to best-in-class MMM for all advertisers, and helps them build their models faster and in a more budget-friendly fashion.

When BARK started building MMM in Q3 2021, it was able to use the Robyn codebase to build its first model in about three months. The company has been using Robyn ever since to cost-efficiently glean the data-driven insights it needs to inform its budget allocation decisions, and help maximize return on ad spend across all channels.

### Impact

According to BARK\*, its Robyn-powered MMM study has allowed the dog-centric company to:

- Generate a 30% increase in overall subscriptions with optimized budget allocation, guided by the MMM analysis
- Efficiently increase spend in key media channels to drive subscription growth, based on opportunities identified by the MMM analysis
- Build out its first in-house MMM analysis in just three months using Meta’s open-source MMM solution, Robyn

*\*Results are self-reported and not identically repeatable. Generally expected individual results may differ.*
